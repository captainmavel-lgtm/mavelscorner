You may not have been told by anybody that following God could lead you somewhere this quiet and this dry, this far from the green pastures everyone talks about in church, because the truth is, you expected the promise to come with a little more scenery along the way. Instead, you have been walking through what feels like sand for months now, watching the horizon stay exactly the same distance away no matter how many steps you take, wondering whether you missed a turn somewhere or whether this barren stretch was always part of the plan.

The Israelites knew this exact kind of disorientation, the kind where you have already left the place that was killing you but have not yet arrived at the place God promised, stuck somewhere in between that nobody prepared you for. They wandered through actual desert for forty years, and yet Deuteronomy makes a statement that should reshape how you see your own wilderness season.

> *“For the Lord your God has blessed you in all the work of your hand. He knows your trudging through this great wilderness. These forty years the Lord your God has been with you, you have lacked nothing.” Deuteronomy 2:7 NKJV*

Read that slowly, because it says something most people miss when they are in the middle of their own desert. God was not waiting at the destination for them to finally arrive. He was already there in the wandering, present in every dry mile, even when it looked from the outside like nothing was happening and nobody was watching.

Think about what that means for the season you are walking through right now, whatever shape your desert has taken. May be it is a job that still has not opened up, or a relationship that remains unresolved no matter how much you have prayed, or a health diagnosis stretching on far longer than you imagined. The temptation in seasons like this is to assume that because nothing visible is changing, God has quietly stepped back and left you to figure it out alone, but the wilderness was never proof of His absence even when it felt that way to an entire generation who walked it for four decades. Something is always being formed in you during the waiting that could never have been formed any other way.

This is why you are being reminded that on the days the silence feels heaviest, know that God has not given up on you simply because your circumstances have not shifted the way you hoped by now. He has not forgotten the prayer that feels unanswered, and the dryness of this season is not evidence that He has wandered off while you keep trudging forward alone. The same God who sustained an entire nation through forty years of desert, providing manna every morning and water from rock, is fully capable of sustaining you through whatever wilderness you are currently walking, because His character has not changed even though your circumstances have not yet resolved.

Waiting in the desert and giving up in the desert are not the same decision, even though exhaustion can make them feel identical on the days you are tired enough to want both at once. Waiting means you are still moving forward even when you cannot see the destination, still choosing to trust God even it feels like the harder option. Isaiah speaks directly into seasons like this one.

> *“But those who wait on the Lord shall renew their strength, they shall mount up with wings like eagles, they shall run and not be weary, they shall walk and not faint.” Isaiah 40:31 NKJV*

The renewal comes through the waiting itself rather than after it ends, which means the strength you need for today is already available even in the middle of a season that has not concluded yet.

So if you find yourself in a desert season right now, be it financial, relational, physical, or spiritual, know that the same presence that walked with the Israelites through their wilderness is walking with you through yours, even on the days it feels like you are completely alone out here. He did not lead you out of what was killing you only to abandon you in the middle of the journey. He is forming something in this season, that comfortable seasons could never have formed, and the dryness you feel right now is not the end of your story but one part of a longer journey toward everything He has already promised you.

## Something To Think About.

What has this current season of waiting been teaching you that you might not have learned any other way, even if you cannot fully see the purpose of it yet?
Where have you mistaken God's quietness for God's absence, and what would change in how you walk through this season if you believed He was present in the silence rather than missing from it?
Who walked through their own desert season and came out the other side, and what would it look like to reach out to them this week instead of carrying this alone?

## Prayer.

God, this season feels dry and I am tired of waiting without seeing where this road actually leads, but I am choosing to believe You have not left me out here by myself. Remind me that the silence does not mean Your absence, and that You have been sustaining me even on the days I could not feel it. Give me strength to keep walking forward instead of sitting down in discouragement, and help me trust that this wilderness is leading somewhere good even though I cannot see the destination from where I am standing right now. I am trusting You with the parts of this journey that still do not make sense to me.

## Challenge.

This week, identify one specific way God has sustained you through this current season, something small or practical you might have overlooked, and write it down somewhere you will see it daily as a reminder that He has been present in the wilderness all along.
